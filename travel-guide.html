How to Get There

  • By Air: Direct flights from Manila or Cebu to Camiguin Airport (CGM).
  • By Land: From Cagayan de Oro, take a bus/van to Balingoan Port (2–3 hrs).
  • By Ferry: From Balingoan Port to Camiguin’s Benoni Port (1–1.5 hrs).

Best Time to Visit

  • Dry Season (December–May): Best for island hopping, waterfalls, and outdoor trips.
  • Rainy Season (June–November): Waterfalls are stronger, but some trips may depend on weather.
  • Best Time for White Island: Early morning (6AM–9AM) for calm waters and cooler sun.
  • Best Sunset Spots: Sunken Cemetery and Yumbing Coastline.

Top Attractions

  • White Island Sandbar: A pristine powdery-sand islet with a view of Hibok-Hibok Volcano.
  • Mantigue Island Nature Park: Known for crystal-clear waters, snorkeling, and marine sanctuary.
  • Katibawasan Falls: A 250-foot waterfall surrounded by lush forest.
  • Tuasan Falls: A less-crowded freshwater falls with strong flow.
  • Sunken Cemetery: Iconic landmark and diving/snorkeling spot.
  • Sto. Niño Cold Spring: A natural cold-water pool from volcanic springs.
  • Ardent Hot Spring: Warm volcanic spring especially relaxing at night.

Travel Guide & Tips

  • Bring cash — ATMs are limited in some towns.
  • Rent a motorbike (₱500–₱700/day) for easier island travel.
  • Wear reef-safe sunscreen to protect marine life.
  • Check weather forecasts before boat trips to White Island or Mantigue.
  • For hikes like Mt. Hibok-Hibok, register at DENR and bring proper gear.

Things You Need

  • ✔ Sunscreen & sun protection
  • ✔ Waterproof bag & phone case
  • ✔ Extra clothes & quick-dry towel
  • ✔ Reusable water bottle
  • ✔ Cash (many tours require cash payments)
  • ✔ Snorkeling gear (optional but recommended)

Sample 3-Day Itinerary

  • Day 1: Arrival → White Island → Sunken Cemetery Sunset
  • Day 2: Katibawasan Falls → Tuasan Falls → Sto. Niño Cold Spring → Ardent Hot Spring
  • Day 3: Mantigue Island → Walk at Old Church Ruins → Souvenir Shopping → Departure
